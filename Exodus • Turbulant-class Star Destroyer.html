    Characteristics:

    A smaller and more maneuverable variant of Star Destroyer, the Turbulent-class was designed with two hangars opening at the stern of the vessel. The ship's bridge was situated in a tower on top of the main superstructure, like many other Star Destroyers. However, unlike its older cousins, the Turbulent-class did away with the shielding domes, taking a page from the Empires Federation allies by incorporating shielding into the superstructure as opposed to rising above it, a technological advent originally done on the Immobilizer 418 cruiser. This also allowed some measure of shield redundancy. The bridge has a large holo-display built into its design and there is a day cabin available to the flag-officer near the bridge. An emergency docking bay is located astern of the tower. Although it was meant to perform as a "pocket destroyer," the Turbulent-Class still retained much of the impressive weaponry that could be found on other destroyers.

    The class has many crisscrossing maintenance tunnels running throughout the upper portion of the vessel. This makes maintenance easier and also provides a means of escape in case the ship was boarded. Because of the labyrinthine nature of this design, both boarding parties as well as escapees would have a difficult time navigating inside the vessel, unless informed of the specific escape routes that wound through the interior, which has the unintended side effect of establishing a more close-knit crew then on other Star Destroyers. Another design focus was an extensive use of bulkheads throughout the interior, minimizing the casualties and damage in case of a hull breach.


    History:

    The Remnant created the class after the end of the Swarm War, making it out to be a smaller and more agile design than previous Star Destroyers. The Bloodfin was one of these ships and saw action during the Second Battle of Fondor during the Second Galactic Civil War. Gilad Pellaeon liked the design, for it made it easier to familiarize with the crew than on a bigger warship. The Bloodfin was the site of an attempted coup of the Imperial Remnant by forces loyal to Colonel Jacen Solo. Pellaeon was killed onboard the vessel, which was then boarded by Mandalorian Supercommandos and many crewmembers were killed. The vessel and Pellaeon's body were taken into custody by the Maw Irregular Fleet.

    After the Second Galactic Civil War, and with strengthened ties between the Empire and the Galactic Federation, the Empire began producing a greater number of the Turbulent-class Star Destroyers. The ships were cheaper to produce then the massive Imperial or Imperial II-class destroyers, while at the same time being easier to maintain and possessing the technological advances of the last fifty years. They also require a fraction of the crew needed to run the older destroyers. While the older variants are still utilized, the Turbulent-class has given the Empire the ability to respond quickly to sectors as needed, bringing in firepower fitting for the Empire while allowing for an adaptability that was necessary during the wars following the death of Palpatine, but seemingly un-conceived of during the design of the older star destroyers.

    The Ascendant Pride acted as Vincent Mikaru's flagship for his Paramilitary Company, Mikaru Security Solutions. The ship is the last remaining Shirudo-class vessel in active operation, as the class was deemed too expensive to produce. For much of the crafts operational life it remained berthed in orbit of the Planet Eshan where it acted as a foundry for the security company, the ships internal manufactories helping to produce new fighters and components for the company. It wasn't until the incursions of the First Order and the destruction of the Hosnian system where the vessel entered service transporting Vincent around, and acting as a decoy during operations, relying on it's heavy armor, shielding, and weaponry to take on First Order Star Destroyers in hit and run and one on one attacks.

    Class History

    In 5ABY the Galactic Empire fell at the battle of Jakku, shortly after the New Republic signed the Galactic Concordance, bringing in a supposed era of peace. To this effect the Military Disarmament Act was also enacted, an act opposed by the Echani Alliance of the Middle Rim. The Alliance refused to sign or adhere to the act unless an amendment was made that granted the Alliance special dispensation to continue to produce military arms, equipment, and personnel, within certain confines. This was of course met with resistance to which the Alliance threatened to secede. This gave rise to a great deal of tension, till former Senator Leia Organa voiced her support, as well as quietly behind closed doors reminding the Republic that they had just dismantled most of their military and the Echani had, by refusal, become the largest military force in the Middle Rim, with the warrior culture to back it up.

    Eventually the Republic relented, and the Echani Expeditionary Amendment was added allowing the Echani government to act, as they saw it, as the last true bastion of peace in a rapidly complacent world. Even with the amendment however, there were restrictions that the Echani had to follow, ships had to fall under certain size or armament regulations, crew limitations, and countless other red tape barriers. What the Senate expected was to appease the Echani at face value, but bury them in details, what they were reminded of, was that all the way back to four millennia before the Empire, Echan's greatest warriors, their Generals, were also their politicians. Despite the red tape, Echani bureaucracy punched loops holes right through them, spearheading an explosion of military design and development intended to develop ships within the parameters of the agreement, and upgrade grandfathered in vessels, such as the Bellator Battlecruisers that orbited the Motherworld. From this was born the Shirudo-class development project.

    During the Civil war the Echani did not develop or produce their own ship designs, as in the decades since the Empire was founded their ship foundries were forced to change to confine to Imperial design doctrine, however while producing ships of Imperial design for the Rebellion, they learned the benefit of adapting other technologies. For example, the Bellator-class FEV Spirit of Eshan while an Imperial design, features Mon Calamari shield generators and Rendili StarDrive turbolaser blisters. To this end, the designers of the Shirudo aimed to combine Imperial, Mon Cal, and other design mentalities into a single vessel. What was birthed was a vessel over two kilometers long, featuring heavy reinforced armor common of Rendili designs, heavy shielding with redundancies akin to Mon Calamari cruisers, and massive focused engine arrays like those found in Corellian capital ships. These designs were all wrapped in a thin, angular hull that married Rendili dreadnought concepts with Quarren dreadnought concepts, resulting in a ship that looked like it was born of a Providence-class Dreadnought and a Dreadnaught-class Heavy Cruiser. However, one thing it did learn from the Quarren designs, was to heavily shield the open hangar area, in order to avoid weaknesses exploited against the Providence-class in key battles during the Clone Wars.

    The ships armament, while Echani in design, was pulled from Tarkin Doctrine concepts recovered from the war. Massive "Star Battlecruiser" type turrets lined the center of the ship on both the dorsal and ventral hull, designed to strike fear in the enemy, and should that fail, provide massive firepower. Four spinal mounted variations of these were also mounted in the fore of the ship, giving it impressive, fixed forward firepower for pursuing targets. New developments in cryogenic cooling allowed for the development of battleship grade beam turrets designated Yari, as well as Heavy Turbolasers that could fire at standard rates, or slower heavier overcharged blasts for ship-to-ship brawling designated Toraidento. To further round out the ships capability eighteen munition launchers were mounted on either side of the vessel, allowing for horizontal deployment of missiles and torpedoes that could arc forward or downward for bombardment, or fired straight broadside.

    The Echani however decided to correct an issue inherent in Imperial Designs; lack of Starfighter defense. Where ISD Mark II's utilized starfighters for anti-fighter defense, it proved to be lackluster considering the TIE Fighter's lack of capabilities. To this end the ship was equipped with forty point defense guns, featuring similar, albeit smaller cryogenic cooling systems to the larger Yari cannons, this permitted the lasers to fire at an extremely high rate in sustained bursts, coupled with the placement of the cannons intended to give total 360 degree coverage and close work done with now freelance former Rebel Alliance pilots to discover blind spots in the ships resulting in complete overhauls of the ships close-in-weapon-system placement to maximize all possible sightlines.

    The classes initial production and trial runs were a total success, unfortunately, the initial production and development costs were also over budget. This resulted in further production being scrapped in preference for other smaller designs, with the weapons development technology from the project being used to enhance those new designs, and upgrade older ships. The unfinished keels that had already been laid were scrapped and two of the five completed ships, including the initial prototype were sold for scrap. Only three ships, the Monsoon, the Resplendent, and the Kova survived, being bought back by Mikaru Shipyards to act as starfighter production facilities. Of the three the Kova remains in service, while the Monsoon was recristend the Ascendant Pride under Mikaru Security Services. The Resplendent over the three decades was converted into a massive, barely mobile foundry, barely looking like the ship it was with additional scaffolding to facilitate the construction of smaller vessels.
